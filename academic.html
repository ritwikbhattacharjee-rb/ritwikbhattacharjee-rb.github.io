Academic Profile

Scholarship as a foundation for clearer public communication.

My doctoral work is grounded in critical theory, democratic theory and decolonization, with a dissertation tentatively titled “Refusing this Lifeworld: Towards Decolonizing the Settler-Canadian Society.”

This academic background helps me work with complex ideas, rigorous argument, sensitive public questions and interdisciplinary research without flattening their meaning.
