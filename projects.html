Featured Project

Arts Perspectives on AI

A flagship editorial initiative exploring artificial intelligence through interdisciplinary faculty and alumni expertise at UBC Faculty of Arts.

Challenge
Generative AI was rapidly reshaping public debate, but academic expertise across the Faculty of Arts needed a coherent, accessible platform for broader audiences.
Role
Conceived story ideas, identified faculty experts, conducted interviews, researched emerging issues, wrote every published feature and managed the editorial process from concept to publication.
Output
Six published feature stories spanning higher education, music, linguistics, Indigenous data stewardship, journalism ethics and AI policy.
Impact
Featured on UBC channels, expanded to UBC's central Generative AI website, and republished by BC Studies and The Ubyssey. One article became the second most-viewed story on arts.ubc.ca.

Featured Project

JWAM 25th Anniversary

Integrated communications and event marketing for AI in Our World: Shaping Media, Education, and Science, the School of Journalism, Writing, and Media's 25th anniversary public event at UBC Robson Square.

Role
Created the event poster and supported integrated communications across branding, promotion, stakeholder coordination and audience engagement.
Channels
Event communications were shared online within and beyond UBC and with external agencies and community audiences.
Outcome
Delivered a near-capacity public event at UBC Robson Square, connecting institutional storytelling, public programming and event communications.
