Public programming case study

JWAM 25th Anniversary

A landmark public event shaped through strategy, audience-building, stakeholder coordination and a clear communications plan.

Event communications UBC Journalism, Writing, and Media Robson Square Near sold-out attendance

Overview

A public-facing anniversary moment with institutional visibility.

I helped shape and deliver a major anniversary event for UBC Journalism, Writing, and Media, supporting the project from early ideation through public promotion, audience-building and event delivery.

The work required coordination across communications, branding, marketing, event logistics and stakeholder relationships — translating an institutional milestone into a public-facing program that felt timely, accessible and well-attended.

1 Landmark event positioned for broad audience appeal and institutional visibility

Challenge

Turn an anniversary into a compelling public event.

The event needed to do more than mark an internal milestone. It needed a clear public proposition, a strong audience strategy and careful coordination across academic, communications and event priorities.

My role

Strategy, coordination and delivery support.

  • Supported ideation and event direction
  • Coordinated with central communications, brand and marketing partners
  • Supported panelist liaison and stakeholder coordination
  • Developed and coordinated landing-page and promotional needs
  • Created or supported event graphics and audience-building assets
  • Managed QR code and event information workflows

Approach

Build the event as a story, not just a date.

The communications approach treated the anniversary as a narrative opportunity: why the milestone mattered, why the topic was relevant now, and why audiences should make time to attend.

Delivery system

How the work came together

1. Event positioning

Clarified how the anniversary could be presented as a timely public conversation rather than a purely internal celebration.

2. Stakeholder alignment

Worked across communications and event partners to keep messaging, logistics and promotional needs aligned.

3. Audience-building

Supported outreach and promotion designed to drive attendance and make the event visible beyond the immediate unit.

4. Event readiness

Helped coordinate public-facing materials, links, QR-code workflows and event information so the audience experience felt smooth.

Outcome

A near sold-out landmark event.

The result was a highly successful public program with strong attendance and a polished communications experience. The project demonstrated my ability to connect strategy, content, design, stakeholder coordination and public engagement into one coherent event campaign.

  • Near sold-out attendance
  • Coordinated event communications across multiple partners
  • Clear landing-page, promotional and audience information workflows
  • Public-facing milestone positioned with strategic communications value
