Sensitive Engagement Case Study

Islamophobia & Antisemitism Workshop Series

A highly sensitive project requiring careful coordination, communications judgment, stakeholder awareness and trust-building.

Snapshot

Focus
Sensitive public engagement
Role
Project management and communications coordination
Risk profile
High sensitivity, multiple stakeholders
Competencies
Judgment, logistics, discretion, stakeholder care

Challenge

Deliver a sensitive workshop series with care and credibility.

Workshops on Islamophobia and antisemitism require careful project management because the subject matter is personally, politically and institutionally sensitive. The communications challenge is not simply promotion; it is creating the conditions for respectful participation, clear expectations and trusted delivery.

This case study should be finalized with any confidentiality constraints you want to apply before publication.

Approach

Director-level work: coordination, judgment and risk-aware delivery.

  1. 01Define scope

    Clarify objectives, audience, sensitivities, stakeholders and approval pathways.

  2. 02Coordinate stakeholders

    Align participants, facilitators, administrators and communications needs.

  3. 03Manage logistics

    Support scheduling, materials, participant experience and delivery planning.

  4. 04Communicate carefully

    Use language that is accurate, respectful, non-inflammatory and audience-aware.

  5. 05Protect trust

    Prioritize discretion, responsiveness and the integrity of the workshop environment.
