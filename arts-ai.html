Case Study 2.0

Arts Perspectives on AI

How a UBC Faculty of Arts editorial series became a thought leadership platform for public-facing conversations on generative AI.

Snapshot

Client context
UBC Faculty of Arts
Focus
AI, research storytelling, public engagement
Role
Series strategy, interviews, writing, editorial execution
Output
Six published features

Executive Answer

A fast-moving public issue needed a credible interdisciplinary voice.

I developed Arts Perspectives on AI as a strategic editorial platform that translated UBC Arts expertise into accessible, timely and public-facing stories. The result was a six-part thought leadership series that earned institutional amplification, external republication and measurable audience engagement.

01 · Situation

Generative AI had become an urgent institutional communications opportunity.

AI was reshaping public debate across education, creative industries, journalism, language, policy and Indigenous data sovereignty. At the same time, scholars across the Faculty of Arts were well positioned to bring context, critique and nuance to the conversation.

The opportunity was not simply to publish articles. It was to create a recognizable editorial platform that could connect Faculty expertise to wider audiences while strengthening UBC's public voice on one of the defining issues of the moment.

02 · Strategic Challenge

Turn distributed expertise into a coherent public narrative.

  • Identify story angles that were timely, accessible and aligned with Faculty expertise.
  • Translate complex academic perspectives without losing nuance.
  • Build consistency across articles while allowing each discipline to speak in its own voice.
  • Move quickly enough to stay relevant in a rapidly changing AI conversation.
  • Create work strong enough for institutional amplification and external republication.

03 · Strategy

Position UBC Arts as a trusted interdisciplinary voice on AI.

1

Frame the platform

Treat AI as a Faculty-wide issue rather than a technology story, connecting it to education, culture, language, journalism, public policy and Indigenous data stewardship.

2

Build the editorial pipeline

Identify faculty experts and alumni, develop story angles, conduct interviews, draft articles and manage review and publication.

3

Write for reach

Convert academic expertise into clear public-facing narratives that could travel beyond the original Faculty channels.

4

Extend visibility

Connect the series to broader UBC communications opportunities, including UBC's central Generative AI website.

04 · Execution

End-to-end ownership from idea to publication.

Editorial concept
Developed the series as an interdisciplinary thought leadership initiative.
Research and discovery
Mapped emerging AI issues to relevant Faculty expertise and public-interest story angles.
Interviews
Interviewed faculty experts and alumni to surface clear arguments, examples and insights.
Writing and editing
Wrote every published article and managed editorial refinement, review and publication.
Promotion and extension
Helped extend the series through Faculty and UBC-wide communications channels.

05 · Proof of Work

The series created a visible body of published work.

06 · Results

The work travelled beyond its original channel.

Institutional amplification
Articles were featured through UBC channels, including UBC's homepage.
University-wide AI visibility
The series was added to UBC's central Generative AI website.
Audience performance
Indigenous Data Stewardship Stands Against Extractivist AI became the second most-viewed story on arts.ubc.ca.
External recognition
The Indigenous data stewardship article was selected for reprint by BC Studies; the Music and AI article was republished by The Ubyssey.

07 · What This Demonstrates

Communications principles transferable to senior roles.

  • Identify emerging issues before they become crowded communications spaces
  • Turn expert knowledge into accessible institutional storytelling
  • Create editorial platforms, not one-off articles
  • Balance nuance, timeliness and public readability
  • Build work that can be amplified across institutional channels
  • Connect communications execution to reputation-building outcomes
