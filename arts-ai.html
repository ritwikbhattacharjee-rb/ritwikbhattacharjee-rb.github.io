Case Study

Arts Perspectives on AI

A flagship UBC Faculty of Arts thought leadership series translating interdisciplinary research on artificial intelligence into public-facing stories.

Overview

Building public-facing thought leadership on AI.

I created and led the Arts Perspectives on AI editorial series while working as Communications Specialist at the UBC Faculty of Arts. The series brought together faculty and alumni perspectives on generative AI across higher education, music, linguistics, Indigenous data stewardship, journalism ethics and public policy.

Challenge

AI was moving faster than public understanding.

Generative AI had become a major public conversation, but much of the coverage focused on hype, disruption or fear. Across the Faculty of Arts, scholars were already asking deeper questions: What does AI mean for education, music, journalism, language, policy, ethics and Indigenous data stewardship?

The communications opportunity was to create a coherent editorial platform that connected that expertise to public audiences in a timely, accessible and institutionally credible way.

My Role

End-to-end editorial ownership.

Concept & strategy
Identified AI as a strategic communications opportunity and developed the editorial series framework.
Research & story development
Researched emerging AI issues and shaped story angles for different disciplines and audiences.
Interviews
Interviewed faculty experts and alumni to translate specialist knowledge into clear, public-facing narratives.
Writing & editing
Wrote every published feature in the series and managed the editorial process from concept to publication.
Publication & visibility
Published through Faculty of Arts channels and extended the series to UBC's central Generative AI website.
