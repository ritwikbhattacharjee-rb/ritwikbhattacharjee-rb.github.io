Public programming case study

JWAM book launches

Two book-launch programs that transformed scholarly publications into memorable public engagement moments through event direction, audience-building and editorial storytelling.

Event communications UBC Journalism, Writing, and Media Public programming Sold-out / high-engagement launches

Overview

Turning academic book launches into compelling public events.

I supported JWAM (School of Journalism, Writing, and Media) book launches by helping shape the event direction, communications approach and audience proposition so the programs felt accessible, timely and worth attending.

The work required moving beyond a standard academic-event format and positioning each launch around a larger public conversation: Black women’s sonic rhetorics, cultural memory, postcolonial democracy, decolonizing knowledge and scholarly dialogue across disciplines.

Featured launches

Alexis McGee’s From Blues to Beyoncé

A sold-out launch celebrating a book on Black women in music, sonic rhetorics, storytelling and social justice pedagogy. The event became one of UBC Bookstore’s most successful book launches, with all available copies purchased during the launch.

Challenge

Make the events feel timely, accessible and worth attending.

Academic book launches can easily become narrow or internally focused. These events needed stronger public framing, clearer audience value and a format that would attract engagement beyond the most obvious circles.

My role

Event direction, communications strategy and promotion.

  • Helped reshape event direction away from initial proposals
  • Supported public-facing positioning and audience messaging
  • Contributed to landing-page and promotional copy direction
  • Supported social, event and audience-building communications
  • Connected scholarly work to public relevance and institutional storytelling

Approach

Lead with the public conversation, not only the publication.

The communications strategy focused on why the topics mattered, what audiences could expect to gain, and how the events connected scholarly work to broader civic, cultural, media and democratic conversations.

Delivery system

How the work came together

1. Reframe the concept

Shifted the event direction toward a stronger public proposition with clearer audience appeal.

2. Build the audience case

Developed messaging that made the launches relevant to readers, students, alumni, faculty and broader public audiences.

3. Coordinate promotion

Supported promotional needs across web, social and event communications so each launch felt coherent and visible.

4. Extend the story

Used post-event storytelling to document the launches, highlight scholarly impact and create durable web content for JWAM.

Outcome

High-engagement launches with stronger public storytelling value.

The launches demonstrated how thoughtful communications strategy can turn academic programming into public engagement: clear framing, stronger audience motivation and event promotion that makes scholarly work feel timely and accessible.

  • Sold-out / high-engagement book-launch programming
  • Stronger public-facing event positioning
  • Clearer audience-building and post-event storytelling strategy
  • Improved connection between scholarly work and public relevance
